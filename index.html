  • So what exactly makes them different?

      • The main difference between venomous and poisonous is how the toxin enters the body. Venomous animals or plants inject their toxins, while poisonous animals or plants release their toxins when they are eaten or touched.

      • Venom and poison can be differentiated as the way it efects, Venomous means something bit or sting and then the venom entered in your nervous system, where else poisonous means something you consumed or touched which eventualy made its way in your system.

    • Well you can segregate the two by analyzing:

  • Symptoms and Effects:

    • For Venom Exposure

      • Local Reactions:

        Pain: Immediate and severe pain at the site of the bite or sting.
        Swelling and Redness: Localized swelling, redness, and bruising around the affected area.
        Bleeding: Oozing from the wound site.

      • Systemic Reactions:

        Neurological Symptoms: Muscle weakness, difficulty breathing, blurred vision, drooping eyelids, and paralysis.
        Cardiovascular Symptoms: Rapid or irregular heartbeat, low blood pressure, and shock.
        Gastrointestinal Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea.
        Coagulation Issues: Bleeding from gums, blood in vomit, stools, or urine due to impaired blood clotting.

    • For Poison Exposure

      • Gastrointestinal Symptoms:

        Nausea and Vomiting: Common initial signs of many poisonings.
        Abdominal Pain: Cramping or discomfort in the stomach area.
        Diarrhea: Frequent, loose, or watery stools.

      • Neurological Symptoms:

        Dizziness and Weakness: Feeling lightheaded or physically weak.
        Confusion: Difficulty thinking clearly or disorientation.
        Seizures: Uncontrolled muscle contractions.
        Unresponsiveness: Lack of reaction to stimuli; in severe cases, coma.

      • Respiratory Symptoms:

        Difficulty Breathing: Shortness of breath or labored breathing.
        Chest Tightness: A feeling of constriction in the chest.

      • Cardiovascular Symptoms:

        Abnormal Heart Rate: Either too fast or too slow.
        Low Blood Pressure: Leading to faintness or shock.

      • Dermatological Symptoms:

        Burns or Irritation: Especially if the poison was corrosive or came into contact with the skin.
        Pale or Bluish Skin: Indicating poor oxygenation.
